Atlas Coloring Book.

Open the tool, drop in an atlas image, color your labels by hand, then flip to the answer key to compare. Each pair you save becomes a page in your own coloring book, grouped by module, so by exam week you have your full study library in one place. The book is yours, on your device.

Open the Coloring Book

Opens in a new tab. No login needed. Your saved pages stay on your device, so use Export book regularly to back them up.

Quick start

How to use it

  1. Load two images. Drop the unlabeled atlas image into the Practice slot and a labeled version into the Answer Key slot. Both can be from textbook scans, lecture slides, or images you take yourself.
  2. Color in your labels. Pick a pen color, choose a size, and label everything you can identify on the practice image. Use white pen for dark backgrounds and navy or crimson for light.
  3. Flip the key. Click Show key (or press K) to swap the background to the labeled answer key. Your labels stay drawn on top, so you can compare side by side.
  4. Save this page to your book. Click Save this page, name it, pick a module (Skeletal, Muscular, etc.), and add notes if you want. The page lives in your coloring book index below the canvas.
  5. Practice with random shuffle. When you are ready to drill, scroll to your book and press Shuffle a page to load a random page from any module or DOK level you filter by.

Study tips

Get the most out of it

  • Label from memory first. Resist the urge to flip the key early. The struggle is where the learning happens. This is the given, not Googled rule we live by in lab.
  • Build modules as we go. When we finish skeletal, your Skeletal module should hold every image we covered. By Week 8, your book is your exam study guide.
  • Export weekly. Your book lives in this browser. If you switch devices or clear browser data, you lose it. Hit Export book at the end of each week and save the JSON file to Drive or email it to yourself.
  • Shuffle for retrieval practice. Open Shuffle a Page the night before lab. Random retrieval beats re-reading every time.
